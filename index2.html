Gochujang

Gochujang is a fermented red pepper paste made of rice, red chili pepper, sweetener and soybean. Gochujang is spicy and has a deep, rich flavor that is pungent and loaded with umami. It is essential to Korean cuisine and extremely versatile -- as a finishing sauce, a soup base or by itself as a condiment. Gochujang has a thick consistency due to its rice base and is deeper in flavor than most hot sauces due to its long fermentation period.

Doenjang

Doenjang is a fermented soybean paste made of crushed soybeans that are first formed into blocks, dried and fermented. The blocks are then placed in salt water to continue fermenting. When ready, the solids are separated to make doenjang.

Ganjang

Ganjang is Korean soy sauce. Ganjang is the liquid that the soybean blocks (doenjang) were immersed in to be fermented. After the solids are separated to make doenjang, the remaining liquid is ganjang.
